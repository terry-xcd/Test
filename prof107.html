Turi-7 - In Memoriam Edith Turi

 

 

Cure Characterization of Readily Available Epoxies by Modulated Temperature DSC

 

Lawrence Judovits, Arkema - larry.judovits@arkema.com
R. Bruce Prime, primethermosets.com - rbprime@sbcglobal.net

 

The ability of modulated temperature DSC (MTDSC) to separate the glass transition, including vitrification, from the cure exotherm makes it ideally suited to monitor thermoset cure. In this presentation we report the cure behavior of readily available epoxies: five-minute epoxy, thirty-minute epoxy and sixty-minute epoxy resins. Our intention is to provide examples of thermoset characterization that can be easily duplicated.

The figure below shows the five-minute epoxy immediately after mixing and after a five minute cure at 25 ?C. It can be seen to be quite complex, with one large exotherm and two shallow exotherms between -20 ?C and ~90 ?C. After curing five minutes at 25 ?C the main exotherm at ~18 ?C is nearly complete, but the Tg has only advanced from -25 ?C to just under 0 ?C. This is still well short of the final Tg of over 30 ?C, which is not reached until 3 hours at 25 ?C. The sixty-minute epoxy is less complex, characterized by a single exotherm. In this presentation we will illustrate the cure of these epoxies by both nonisothermal and quasi-isothermal DSC (QiDSC).

Fig.1. MTDSC of five-minute epoxy, immediately after mixing and after a five minute cure at 25 ?C. The MTDSC parameters used were a 2 °C/min heating rate with an amplitude of ± 0.212 ºC and a period of 40s.

Dedicated to the memory of Professor Edith Turi.
